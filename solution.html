Solution

Overhaul The Planning Process

Overhaul the planning process for constructing new buildings since there are too many restrictions in its current state (along with too much power in the hands of the general public to immediately stop the construction of new infrastructure). With this in place, the construction of affordable housing will be a much more efficient and easy process, allowing for it to be more successful overall and improve the lives of families all over San Francisco.

Attain More Land

Attain more land for new (affordable) housing by removing wealthy suburban homes and excess land from tech companies, and relocating its residents via a “seasteading” approach (where they would be situated along the outskirts of San Francisco near/ on the water through floating platforms), while using the land that has been cleared for affordable, residential housing facilities. In addition, the lure of “living on water” would be effective in driving up prices in the seasteading area to turn it into a luxury item, causing the people previously in 2-story houses along with higher-income residents to want to stay there. This would be necessary to ensure that there is enough room for all of the affordable housing units that we plan to build, and therefore help to reduce the financial burden that San Francisco house prices place on its citizens.

Create SRO Housing

Create housing out of more economically-minded materials, along with the construction of Single Room Occupancy housing (also known as Micro-units) to preserve space and accommodate more individuals. With this in place, more people would be able to be housed in San Francisco (in an affordable way), while eliminating the unnecessary need of a plethora of rooms, when a single one (such as the case is in an apartment) does more than enough.

Create A Public Relations Campaign

Create a public relations campaign to get the public more accepting of extending height limits on buildings, removing past restrictions, etc. along with convincing people who are opposed to change (such as those who suffered from gentrification) to be more understanding of the project and its beneficial results. The final stage of this sub-idea would be to put in place new air regulation/ environmentally beneficial devices to gain more support even in the face of new construction. This would be necessary to make sure that the public fully understands the meaningful changes that we are bringing to San Francisco, rather than dismissing them out of ignorance.

Preserve Rent Control

Preserve rent control on housing that already exists in order to create a stable environment and make sure that renting prices are accurately (and appropriately) set. New regulations or other laws concerning this will also be created if needed, to make sure that the consumer is not priced out of the market. With this in place, tenants and other citizens living in our affordable houses would be fully protected, and not have to suffer like victims of the Ellis Act.

Build More Transportation Systems

Build more transportation systems and fortifying those that already exist to allow for people to live in nearby suburbs, but commute to San Francisco- alleviating some of the stress on the housing crisis. This would be necessary in order to provide citizens with many different options on where exactly they would like to live while commuting to San Francisco, giving more power to the people rather than allowing the greedy house market to take advantage of consumers.

Increase Taxes On Tech Companies

Increase taxes on tech companies to a more reasonable level though the concept of a gross receipts tax (taxing the profits of companies rather than its employee’s payrolls), in order to help provide enough money to construct all of the affordable housing. In addition, government/ state subsidies would be used to help fund some of the housing facilities, along with helping to pay for each individual’s rent in order to make it more affordable to the average citizen living in the area. With this in place, people will have the ability to truly achieve their dream of living in an affordable housing unit, and not be left empty handed by false promises.
